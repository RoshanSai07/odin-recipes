Pulihora (Tamarind Rice)

Description

Pulihora, also known as tamarind rice, is a popular Andhra-style rice dish that balances tangy, spicy, and slightly sweet flavors. Traditionally prepared during festivals and special occasions, it is easy to make and great for travel or lunchboxes.

The tamarind pulp, tempered spices, curry leaves, and crunchy peanuts come together to make this a beloved comfort dish in Telugu households.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (cooled)
  • 3 tablespoons thick tamarind pulp
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1 green chili, slit
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons roasted peanuts
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Cook rice and allow it to cool completely, spreading it out to prevent sticking.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chana dal, urad dal, red chilies, green chili, and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add curry leaves, turmeric powder, asafoetida, and roasted peanuts.
  5. Add the thick tamarind pulp and cook for a few minutes until it thickens slightly.
  6. Turn off the heat and mix this tamarind seasoning with the cooled rice gently.
  7. Allow the flavors to blend by resting for 15–20 minutes before serving.
